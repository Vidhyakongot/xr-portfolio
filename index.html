AR Training System - Industrial Machine Maintenance

Master's Thesis · TH Deggendorf, in cooperation with Robert Bosch GmbH · Oct 2025 - Mar 2026

Completed

Context

Paper-based maintenance instructions are genuinely dangerous for the right task: a 42-step wheel-change procedure where operators mentally translate 2D diagrams into 3D actions, performed infrequently enough that memory doesn't reliably carry over - and where a mistake at high speed is a safety incident, not an inconvenience.

Challenge

Replace the paper manual entirely with an iPad-based AR training app, without manufacturer CAD data available for the machine, and without slowing operators down relative to the process they already knew.

My Role

Sole designer and developer. Owned the full pipeline - 3D reconstruction, AR interaction design, safety-gate logic, and on-site testing - and reported findings directly to Bosch management.

Process

  • Hybrid 3D reconstruction - LiDAR scanning + Gaussian splatting + AI tools (Reality Scan, Polycam, SAM 3D, Meshy AI), refined in Blender into an AR-ready model with zero CAD input.
  • AI-based PPE safety gate using GPT-4o-mini to verify vest, gloves, and boots before unlocking the procedure, at 3-5 second detection latency.
  • 42-step guided AR walkthrough across four phases - preparation, disassembly, new-part preparation, reassembly - anchored with LiDAR-based plane detection.

Tools

SwiftARKitRealityKitXcodeBlender LiDAR ScanningGaussian SplattingReality ScanPolycam SAM 3DMeshy AIGPT-4o-mini

Validation

Tested three hypotheses in an on-site expert evaluation: AR enables correct task completion without a paper manual, the hybrid pipeline produces operator-recognizable models without CAD data, and GPT-4o-mini reliably handles PPE detection under real factory conditions. All three were validated - all 42 steps completed without the manual, models confirmed clearly recognizable, and added crane animation and voice guidance improved clarity.

Outcome

Findings translated into concrete recommendations for Bosch's manufacturing training strategy, delivered through technical documentation and management workshops.
